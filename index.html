    Timeline of Robin's life:

  • 1951 - Born in Chicago.
  • 1969 Graduated from Redwood High School - Noted to be somewhat of a class clown.
  • 1973 - Received a full ride scholarship to Juilliard School in New York City.
  • 1977 - Robin was spotted performing stand-up comedy at the Comedy Club in Los Angeles by George Schlatter and asked to be on his 'Laugh-in' show.
  • 1978 - Auditioned successfully for the roll of Mork the Alien in the show 'Happy Days'. This was also the year he married his first wife Valarie Velardi whom he had a son with.
  • 1979 - He won a Grammy Award for his comedy show, 'Reality... What a Concept'.
  • 1980 - He made his big break playing Popeye the Sailor Man in 'Popeye'.
  • 1984 - Robin found himself playing Vladimir Ivanoff in comedy-drama 'Moscow on the Hudson'. He was nominated for the Golden Globe Award for Best Actor.
  • 1989 - Appeared in 'Dead Poets Society', a film about an English teacher in a conservative academy who inspires his students by teaching them poetry. He also married Marsha Garces, his second wife.
  • 1993 - One of his most memorable comedy films 'Mrs. Doubtfire'.
  • 1997 - Robin receieved the opportunity to star in the brilliant movie, 'Good Will Hunting' in which he played a therapist who counsels a troubled yet talented young man. This movie won many awards.
  • 2011 - His third marriage with Susan Schneider.
  • 2014 - Robin Williams died at the age of 63 on August 11.
